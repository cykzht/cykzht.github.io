Farewell to TuitionDrop

发表于2023-12-22|更新于2025-08-07|英文期刊
|总字数:671|阅读时长:4分钟|浏览量:

Introduction

Time flies, and the year 2023 is drawing to a close. TuitionDrop was launched on March 21, 2022, and has been operational for nearly two years.

TuitionDrop allows users to bypass the restrictions of certain educational platforms and access their actual exam scores.

The creator of TuitionDrop was a high school senior who, unable to see their own scores, struggled to adjust their study pace. Therefore, they launched the TuitionDrop program during the second half of their senior year, checked their scores, and made timely adjustments, ultimately getting admitted to a reputable 985 university in the national college entrance exam. The program was originally developed for personal use but later found that many students had a strong need for score checking. Thus, it was published as a web version to benefit more people.

The original creator shut down the program after their college entrance exam. Our company communicated with the original creator, obtained the website’s source code and the use rights of a secondary domain, and continued to help students check their scores. During our operation, we have made multiple optimizations to the program’s main framework, added features for checking historical scores, improved the ranking display, and made corresponding optimizations for high-concurrency scenarios.

Shutdown

Due to the tightening of the “Double Reduction” policy, the service of score checking has faced severe pressure, and the “record filing” policy for internet applications has raised questions about the compliance of platforms like TuitionDrop, which function as “cheat codes.”

After careful consideration, TuitionDrop has decided to shut down on December 31, 2023, at 00:00. At that time, the domain

will be inaccessible, and the score checking feature on the WeChat official account will also be unavailable (other features will remain unaffected).

We fully understand that the shutdown of TuitionDrop will be a significant blow to many students. However, as a non-profit public welfare project, we lack the resources to continue maintaining it.

How to Check Scores in the Future?

TuitionDrop is an open-source project, and the source code can be obtained on GitHub. The program is fully integrated, requiring no programming knowledge to set up your own score-checking website.

On December 31, 2023, we will make a final commit to TuitionDrop, and future updates will be indefinitely postponed. If this code has been helpful to you, we hope you can give it a star to let more people discover this project.

This project is only compatible with devices that support the Python environment. If you are using a mobile device, please refer to “Zhixue: Easily Check Scores on Your Phone.”

Final Thoughts

Learning is a major event in life. To avoid being bullied, one must arm oneself with knowledge; to change one’s destiny, one must use knowledge to do so.

After the promulgation of the “double reduction” policy, students have less time to study, less things they have learned, and some students with poor foundations have lost the opportunity to go to tutoring classes to counterattack, so that some children from civilian families can never turn over, and these people who formulate the policy say that they want to be patriotic, and send their children abroad to study with their backhands, wanting their own children to learn better, and not wanting their children to compete too fiercely, is this their so-called educational fairness?

Zhixue.com officially closed the inquiry of scores is also a helpless move, don’t fantasize about what changes a small company can bring, between the nonsense development of education policy in recent years, we should express our understanding of Internet companies engaged in this industry. All students must study hard, master more knowledge, surpass the children of those leaders, and repay their children in the future for the mistakes made by their parents at the moment.

If a dropout network falls, more dropout websites will stand up, and we must fight the policy to the end!
